America I

In late August 2015, I embarked on one of the coolest things I think I’ve ever done in my short little life. An exciting career opportunity moved me from North Carolina to California, and I decided to turn the relocation process into a bucket list moment—the dream cross-country road trip.

Week one brought me all the way from my East Coast hometown to Boulder, Colorado. Stops along the way included Asheville, Louisville, and St. Louis. Every step of the way brought new adventures—I drove misty gravel roads in the Smokies to see Max Patch Mountain, chatted up some of the world’s highest-paid (and most pampered) professional athletes at Churchill Downs, drank Budweiser on nitro at the massive and perfectly American Anheuser-Busch headquarters, and Chautauqua’ed my way up the Flatirons at sunset.

The first leg of my great American road trip ended with a 13-hour stretch across the heart of the country to land me in Boulder. My mom joined me and my sister in St. Louis and helped me power through the dreaded Kansas drive in one day. The fly-over state, I have to say, was astoundingly beautiful. Miles and miles of rolling golden fields lined Highway 70 the entire way with no interruptions except for the occasional silo or wind farm, which actually seriously enhanced the view. Farmland stretched on forever, and the sky stretched on forever times two. We made it to Boulder before sundown and lugged our bags into a totally 70s cylindrical mountain house/art gallery combination before eating a quick dinner and collapsing into our beds, needing to rest up before a couple days of exploring the mile-high city’s slightly higher counterpart.
