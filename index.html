How Did I Get Here

Monica Poletti is a software engineer by trade and an adventurer at heart. Her professional strengths, interests, and experiences revolve primarily around vehicle control and navigation, computer vision, mathematics, and human-computer interaction. She’s inspired by projects that can impact and improve health and safety and highly values usability and simplicity in design.

Raised in Morehead City, North Carolina, Monica later moved to Durham and Raleigh for school. In 2014, she moved to Burlington, Vermont to work on navigation for autonomous underwater robots (interested?). When she’s not working, she's likely hunting down a new restaurant to try or honing her skills at the rock climbing gym. If she’s anywhere with elevation, she’ll probably be hiking or climbing to the best views or on skis getting enthusiastically lost off-piste. Off the mountain, she also feels right at home in the desertscape of the American Southwest.

Monica enjoys eating and attempting to cook Indian food, learning about motorcycles and vehicle restoration, and reading realism and nonfiction. She is influenced by, among countless others, Nina Garcia and April Ludgate. Monica currently resides in her beloved Subaru, Joe Biden, but will soon be returning to a lifestyle of permanent residence and full-time employment in San Francisco and Mountain View.
