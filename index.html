Work (Geekery)

From January through August of 2014, I worked in Richmond, Vermont as a full-time intern at an underwater robotics company called Greensea Systems. Greensea designs products to help people easily and intuitively drive and operate their ROVs and AUVs from their computer rather than a joystick or control box. My job involved merging computer vision and sonar data to develop functional control features that rely solely on a single piece of sonar equipment, a very cost effective (and we found often more reliable) solution for underwater navigation.

Prior to Greensea, I worked on a research project at the Stanford University Department of Geophysics. My project involved writing programs to process a lot of seismic data (read: squiggles) to pick out small earthquakes that would normally go undetected by the human eye. These earthquakes, while not important and barely noticable in everyday life, occur more frequently and closer together than larger ones and have a lot to show about the structure of the earth and how it will change over time.
