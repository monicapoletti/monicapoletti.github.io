Skiing Hokkaido

In December 2014, I had the amazing experience of going to Hokkaido, the north island of Japan, for two weeks of incredible early-season skiing (and equally stunning sushi). My friend and adventure partner, Warren, all but lived out of our rented wagon as we drove around the island sniffing out the most legendary powder in the world. We tried it all, from small town practice hills to party-central Niseko to desolate, terrifyingly cliffy Kurodake (I was actually slightly relieved to discover the more treacherous part of this mountain didn’t have enough snow to ski, so we had to take the tram over it).

In Daisetsuzan National Park, we found our Valhalla—Mount Asahi. The snow there, while comprised of the biggest flakes I’ve ever seen, was about as dense as a cloud. And the best part? It was always falling. Almost the entire mountain is backcountry, so our possibilities were endless, and we ended up staying extra days there for more playtime.

With utmost sincerity, skiing Hokkaido was truly, deeply, one of the most trill experiences of my life. It’s a magical winter paradise—fat ski paradise—and one of the coolest places I've ever been. No doubt I'll be going back.
